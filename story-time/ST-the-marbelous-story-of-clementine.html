The Marbelous Story of Clementine

__________

There once was a girl called Clementine.
Hearing childhood stories from her Teta was how she spent her time.

“Let me tell you the story about how I won the marble tournament...”
Teta would say. But in Clementine’s mind, Teta was the champion who won the World Cup.

One day Clementine came back to visit and hear stories again.
Teta was gone, but she left Clementine her special marble necklace.

The next day Clementine was excited to see her friends.
She told everyone about Teta the champion, and all the great things she had done.

The other kids found the stories boring.
“You want us to listen to these old tales?!”
And then they went back to racing their toy cars.

Clementine left the group sad and angry.
“I wish I never listened to those old stories!”, she shouted and she threw the necklace away.

But the marble necklace didn’t go away.
It bounced, bobbed, skipped and jumped all around.

It bounced so much it made its way to the race and finished first!

The children surrounded a happy and proud Clementine, and cheered, “We love this game you found!”

Everyone wanted to play and get their own marble. Although not everyone could find one...

...but no one cared. Everyone loved playing this new game of old.

And that’s the story of my Teta and I
Now it’s your turn to share old stories and make them your own.

  • champion: a person, team, etc. that has won a competition, especially in a sport
  • proud: feeling pleased and satisfied about something that you own or have done, or are connected with
  • tournament: a sports competition involving a number of teams or players who take part in different games and must leave the competition if they lose. The competition continues until there is only the winner left.
  • tales: stories created using the imagination, especially one that is full of action and adventure
  • surrounded: to be all around something/somebody
  • cheered: to shout loudly, in order to show support or praise for somebody, or to encourage them
  • bounced: moves quickly away from a surface it has just hit or you make it do this
  • skipped: to move forwards lightly and quickly making a little jump with each step
