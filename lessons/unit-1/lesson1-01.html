Notes:

#A: I'm = I am

I'm Kevin. = I am Kevin.
I'm Jim. = I am Jim.

#B: Hi (family, friends, neighbors) = Hello (teachers, boss, important, unknown)

Hi, Kevin. (friend)
Hi, mom. (family)
Hello, Mr. Henderson. (teacher)

#C

  • "And you?" = repeat the question
  • "...,too." = repeat the same answer

A. How old are you?
B. 23. And (how old are) you?
A. I'm 23, too.
B. Where do you live?
A. Dalanzadgad. And you?
B. I live in Dalanzadgad, too.
A. What is your favorite color?
B. Green. And you?
A. My favorite color is green, too.

#D: "Me, too." = want to say same thing

A. I'm hot.
B. Me, too. (I'm hot, too.)
B. I'm going to study.
A. Me, too.
A. You have a good evening.
B. You, too.

#E: "Nice to meet you." = "Nice meeting you."
This is said only the first time you meet someone.

#F:

"My name is Jim Damico.

  • 'Jim' = first name, given name
  • 'Damico' = last name, surname

Mr. [mister] + surname = used for men like teachers, doctors, officials
Mrs. [misses] + surname = used for married women like teachers, doctors, officials
Ms. [mizz] + surname = use for women but do not know if married, like teachers, doctors, officials
Miss [miss] + surname = used for young women not married but you do not know, like a shopkeeper

In Western Countries like the USA, Australia and the UK, if you want to shorten a name, you use the first initial (letter) of the first name. Not the last name!

  • Jim Damico => J. Damico

 

'Wh' Question Words


Who, What, Which, Where, When, Why

Who - We use 'who' to ask about people.

  • Who is your teacher? = Jim bagsh
  • Who helped you? = my friend
  • Who is coming tomorrow? = John and his family

What - We use 'what' to ask about things.

  • What is that? = It is a pencil.
  • What is your name? = My name is Mary.
  • What day is today? = Today is Sunday.

Where - We use 'where' to ask about place.

  • Where is the shop? = It is down the street.
  • Where is my book? = Your book is at home.
  • Where is Mongolia? = Mongolia is in Asia.

When - We use 'when' to ask about time.

  • When is dinner? = Dinner is at 6 o'clock.
  • When will you go? = I will go next summer.
  • When did you see Susan? = I saw Susan yesterday.

Which - We use 'which' ask to choose.

  • Which is easy to learn, English or Chinese? = English.
  • Which do you want, an apple or chocolate? = Chocolate!

Why - We use 'why' to ask because.

  • Why do you want to speak English? = Because everyone speaks English.
  • Why are you tired? = I studied too late.
